## The Drink Nobody Else Buys

The vending machine at the West Regional had exactly one yellow can left.

Ethan Hyun stood in front of it still wearing half his headgear, silver-letter belt crooked under his hogu, gold headphones around his neck instead of on his ears — the first time Yuna Park had seen them off.

He lost in round one. She knew because she saw the spinning kick from the poomsae warm-up area — a blur of someone else's foot, Ethan's body folding wrong, the electronic beep that sounded too loud in her memory even hours later.

She was fifteen. Poomsae athlete. Not his teammate. Not his friend.

Just a girl two rings over who kept noticing the quiet fighter from Kwon's.

---

He fed coins into the machine. *Clink. Clink.*

The can dropped — **Pineapple Fanta** — neon label, ridiculous color, completely wrong for a kid who looked like he wanted to disappear.

He didn't look at the machine like he was choosing.

He looked at it like it was **procedure**.

*Open. Sip. Breathe.*

A small exhale — not happiness. Not sadness. Something in between that only showed on his face for half a second before the mask came back.

**Logan** bounded over from nowhere, too loud for the hallway.

**LOGAN**  
Hyung! They have grape too—

**ETHAN**  
Not the same.

**LOGAN**  
It's both sugar!

**ETHAN**  
*(already walking)*  
Pineapple.

Logan shrugged at the vending machine like it had personally offended him and followed his brother.

Yuna watched the empty space where Ethan had been.

She had never tried Pineapple Fanta. Her mother only bought barley tea and water. Sweet drinks were for birthdays.

That night, on the bus home, she stopped at a corner store and bought one.

Too sweet. Too yellow. Too much.

But when she drank it, she thought about the way Ethan's shoulders dropped one inch after the first sip — like the can was a weight he chose on purpose.

She didn't throw it away.

She wrote in the margin of her poomsae notebook, small letters:

> *The quiet boy from sparring — pineapple soda after he loses. Remember.*

## What She Learned Later (Arc 5)

Months later, Kwon's hosted a **poomsae vs. sparring rules clinic**. Yuna came to volunteer — bracket runner for the forms side.

Ethan was there translating pamphlets for younger kids. Headphones on. Patient.

Logan saw her staring at the empty Fanta can in the recycling bin by Ethan's bag.

**LOGAN**  
*(helpful, zero filter)*  
Oh — that's Hyung's victory drink! Also his losing drink. Also his Tuesday drink.

**YUNA**  
*(careful)*  
…Pineapple Fanta?

**LOGAN**  
Only the yellow one! Don't get the wrong flavor or he makes this face—

He imitated Ethan's disapproval. Yuna laughed before she could stop herself.

Ethan looked up. Headphones slid one ear off.

**ETHAN**  
What?

**YUNA**  
*(too fast)*  
Nothing. Rules pamphlet. Section four.

She held up paper like a shield.

He nodded. Headphones back on.

Logan whispered anyway:

**LOGAN**  
Halmeoni used to buy it for him after school. Before hospital. Now he buys it himself. Don't tell him I told you.

Yuna didn't tell.

She just **remembered better**.

## Korea (Arc 6) — Almost

She packed a Pineapple Fanta in her tournament bag for Ethan before the Korea flight.

She never gave it to him.

He lost on points in round three — controlled, proud, still not enough — and she watched from the poomsae stands with her fingers on the cold can until it warmed and went flat.

*Not yet,* she thought. *Wrong moment. Not a rescue drink. A see-you-tomorrow drink.*

She drank it herself on the bus back to the hotel.

Too sweet.

She still didn't throw it away.
