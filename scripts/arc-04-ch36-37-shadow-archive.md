**Panels:** 36.0 bridge

# MEMORY CASCADE [REAL #10 · #24 · #9 · #25–27]

### SCENE 2 — BOARD STATION ★
**[FLASH — STYLE: PRIME]**

Smallest in testing group. Adult board — **too high, too far** for his frame.

**TTONG KIM**  
Step up. Punch through. Don't embarrass the school.

*Thud. Thud. Thud.* Wrong angle — below center line — board flexes. Clock moves.

**PARENT C (O.S.)**  
Why don't they **move the board closer**?

**TTONG KIM** sips coffee. **Says nothing.** Won't correct stance. Won't tell volunteer to lower board.

**MOTHER (O.S.)**  
Can he stop? He already—

**TTONG KIM**  
He'll break it or he won't. Sit down.

**TTONG KIM**  
Then let him learn what quitting looks like.

**Almost an hour.** Ethan **shifts foot forward** — hip rotates — fist **finally** finds center.

Board splits — Ethan stumbles — hand **black and blue**.

**TTONG KIM (disgust)**  
Late and ugly isn't the same as good. **One out of five.**

Parents **crying** — **whispers after break**:

**PARENT D (O.S.)**  
Wrong spot the whole time until he **adjusted himself**.

**PARENT E (O.S.)**  
Coach should've said **one word**.

Results posted — Ethan **highest pass** — Ha-eun **bonus** — Ethan **#2**.

**TTONG KIM**  
First place — Ha-eun. Second — Ethan. Both pass.

**ETHAN (V.O.)**  
It broke when I moved. Not when they helped. I would have passed with zero.

### SCENE 5 — PRESENT SNAP
**Panels:** 37.6 bridge

**ACTION — STYLE: KWONS**

Ethan on mat — breathing hard — not crying.

Footsteps — **MV** — coat on — came back for keys — stops.

**MV**  
You shouldn't be alone with that memory.

**ETHAN**  
I thought if I said it once at the tournament, it would leave.

**MV**  
It doesn't leave. You **learn where it lives** so it doesn't drive.

Beat.

**MV**  
At Kwon's, promotion is timestamped. Same criteria. Boys and girls.

**ETHAN**  
She would've had three belts by then.

**MV**  
And you would've had none of the trust that came with them.

Ethan stands. Bows to MV — not performance — gratitude.

**MV**  
Go home. Sleep. Tomorrow we train the **second beat**, not the first wound.
