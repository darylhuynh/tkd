# Flashback — The Board Break Hour
## *REAL #24 · Premier Taekwondo Belt Test*

**Character:** Ethan Hyun (young teen) · **Ttong Kim** · favored student (composite) · crowd/parents  
**Placement:** **Arc 3, Chapter 28** (flashback during Ch 25 loss) · extended **Arc 4, Chapter 36**  
**Tone:** Not triumph — exhaustion, institutional cruelty, favoritism  
**Panels:** [arc-03-panel-storyboard.md](../storyboards/arc-03-panel-storyboard.md) §28.1–28.7 · [arc-04-panel-storyboard.md](../storyboards/arc-04-panel-storyboard.md) §36.0–36.2

---

## Context

Black belt testing day at Premier. Ethan is the **smallest and youngest** in his testing group. The board break station is scored **out of 5**. A **zero still passes** the overall test.

He does not know that yet.

---

### SCENE — PREMIER DOJANG · TESTING DAY · MORNING
**Panels:** 28.1

**ACTION:**  
Gymnasium chairs in rows. Parents with cameras. Testing groups called by belt color.

Ethan — white dobok, thin wrists — stands at the **board break line** behind two older teens who broke on the first or second try.

**TTONG KIM**  
*(flat)*  
Step up. Punch through. Don't embarrass the school.

The board holder is a parent volunteer. The board looks **too thick** for Ethan's frame.

Ethan bows. Chambers fist.

**Punch.**

*Thud.*

Board holds.

---

### SCENE — TWENTY MINUTES LATER
**Panels:** 28.2

**ACTION:**  
Same spot. Knuckles **pink**. Ethan resets stance. Punch. *Thud.*

Wrong angle again — fist hitting **below** the center line — board flexes, doesn't snap.

**TTONG KIM**  
*(to another parent — not Ethan)*  
Good power. Needs commitment.

He does **not** look at Ethan's feet. Does **not** tell the volunteer to **lower the board**. Does **not** say *step closer* or *rotate your hip*.

Next tester in another group finishes. Gets a nod. Walks off.

Ethan again.

*Thud.*

**ETHAN (V.O.)**  
Other kids went to forms. Sparring. I was still here.  
Punching the wrong place. Nobody said **where**.

---

### SCENE — FORTY MINUTES
**Panels:** 28.3

**ACTION:**  
Wall clock: hands moved. Parents in Ethan's row **standing now** — not cheering — **worried**.

**MOTHER (O.S.)**  
*(to instructor)*  
Can he— can he stop? He already—

**TTONG KIM**  
*(without looking at her)*  
He'll break it or he won't. Sit down.

**FATHER (O.S.)**  
He'd pass the test anyway—

**TTONG KIM**  
Then let him learn what quitting looks like.

Ethan hears none of it cleanly. Just punch rhythm.

*Thud. Thud. Thud.*

**PARENT C (O.S.) — whisper, not to Ethan)**  
Why don't they **move the board closer**? He's too short for that height—

**PARENT D (O.S.)**  
Shh. Not our kid.

**PARENT C**  
He's not even hitting the **center**. Look at his fist line—

Ethan keeps punching. Same wrong line. **Ttong Kim** sips coffee. **Says nothing.**

A girl two rows over — the one who always laughed at the instructor's jokes — watches with **pity**, not cruelty. That might be worse.

---

### SCENE — THE BREAK
**Panels:** 28.4

**ACTION:**  
**Almost an hour** — story time, not stopwatch dialogue.

Ethan stumbles — resets — **shifts one foot forward** — adjusts hip — **finally** lines fist with the board's center — not because a coach told him. Because his body **ran out of wrong answers**.

**Punch.**

Board **splits** — crack echo — his body follows through too far — stumbles to knees.

Not a cheer. A few uncomfortable claps.

**TTONG KIM**  
*(flat — to grader)*  
Finally.

He still doesn't say *good adjustment*. Doesn't say *should've been thirty seconds*.

He looks at his right hand — **swollen**, **black and blue**, trembling.

**ETHAN (V.O.)**  
It broke when I **moved**.  
Not when they **helped**.  
They never helped.

---

### SCENE — GRADING TABLE
**Panels:** 28.5

**ACTION:**  
Ethan holds injured hand to chest. Grader marks sheet.

**GRADER**  
Board break… **one out of five.**

**TTONG KIM**  
*(leaning in — quiet — Ethan hears)*  
You made everyone wait. One point.

Not yelling. **Disappointment.** Like Ethan personally failed a math test that didn't matter.

**ETHAN**  
*(small)*  
I broke it.

**TTONG KIM**  
Late and ugly isn't the same as good.

---

### SCENE — BLEACHERS
**Panels:** 28.6

**ACTION:**  
Ethan walks to hydration table. Parents in the crowd — **some crying** — **whispering louder now that it's over**.

**PARENT A (O.S.)**  
Why didn't someone **stop** him?

**PARENT B (O.S.)**  
He would've passed without it…

**PARENT C (O.S.) — the one who noticed earlier)**  
Why didn't they **move the board closer**? He was **too small** for that setup the whole time.

**PARENT D (O.S.)**  
He wasn't punching the **right spot** until the end. Did you see when he **stepped in**? That's when it broke.

**PARENT E (O.S.)**  
The instructor should've said **one word**. Adjust. Step. **Anything**.

**PARENT A**  
Then why—

**FATHER (O.S.) — Ethan's, hoarse, not looking at Ttong Kim)**  
…He figured it out **himself**. After an hour.

Ethan keeps walking. Head down. Hand throbbing. Heard every word.

---

### SCENE — RESULTS POSTED
**Panels:** 28.7 · [REAL #9]

**ACTION:**  
Large printout: **PASSING SCORES**

Ethan's name — **top overall** among passers. His chest lifts half an inch.

Then Ttong Kim adds a line at the bottom — **bonus points** — **attitude / school spirit** — to the girl who finished board break in three tries and spent the rest of the hour **sitting on his instructor's side of the floor**, smiling.

Her total moves **above** Ethan's.

**#1** → crossed out mentally.  
**#2**.

**TTONG KIM**  
*(to group)*  
First place — **Ha-eun.** Second — Ethan. Both pass. Be proud.

The girl hugs his arm. Instructor pats her shoulder.

Ethan stands with swollen hand in pocket. Highest score. Second place. Same mat.

**ETHAN (V.O.)**  
I would have passed with zero on the board.  
I know that now.  
I didn't know it then.

---

### SCENE — CEREMONY (BRIDGE TO CH 28.8)
**Panels:** 28.8 · [REAL #10]

**ACTION:**  
Black belt tied. Photos. Parents proud.

Ethan's hand hidden in sleeve. Smile for camera — **doesn't reach eyes**.

**ETHAN (V.O.)**  
Nothing left but fear.
